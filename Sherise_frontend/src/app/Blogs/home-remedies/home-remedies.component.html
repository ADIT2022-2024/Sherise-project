Home Remedies for Menorrhagia

Answering questions like “how to reduce bleeding during periods?” might be a little difficult and one that we would suggest you ask help for from a health professional, but we can certainly offer some tips to help you manage your menstrual cramps effectively. We have put together some simple home remedies for women with menorrhagia (aka heavy flow period) that you can try from the comfort of your home. Let’s dig in, shall we?

1. Superfoods for Superwomen

It may be difficult to get your hands on exotic, herbal ingredients, so try including these readily available superfoods from your kitchen into your diet, which is surely the best home remedy for heavy periods:

  • Turmeric: Having a warm cup of water infused with turmeric soothes cramps by increasing the blood flow to the uterus and pelvic region.
  • Seeds: Eating soaked fenugreek, cumin, and flax seeds during periods has shown to reduce inflammation and pain considerably.
  • Tea: Peppermint, chamomile, and tea with black pepper have pain-relieving and calming effects which instantly put you in a good mood.

You can consume these foods raw, soaked in water, or with your meals, and adding these to your diet will make a huge difference. While the above-mentioned food items are ideal home remedies for heavy periods, avoiding certain foods will also help regulate your pain better.

2. Hydrate, Hydrate, Hydrate

Have you ever felt like a bloated puffer fish on your period? Well, you are not alone! One of the best home remedies to combat bloating is: Drink more water! While you are menstruating, hormonal changes cause your body to retain more salt and water, which could lead to bloating. So bottoms up!

3. Stay Active

Contrary to popular belief, staying active is a natural way to improve blood circulation and uplift your mood. Light cardio exercises like walking or doing yoga will increase your heartbeat and release a rush of endorphins in your body. If that’s not your thing, you could give yourself an acupuncture massage with your favorite essential oils, which is also a great home remedy for relief from heavy period pain!

4. Self-Care Is the Best Care

Wouldn’t you love to escape from a world of pain and spend time doing things that make you happy & divert your mind from the heavy flow period? Well, you can! You could listen to your favorite tunes in the comfiest of sweats, light a scented candle or even take that relaxing bubble bath you’ve been thinking about all week. The sky’s the limit!

5. Do-It-Yourself Time

Sure, masturbating on your period can get quite messy, but it’s not without benefits of its own. When you orgasm, your body releases certain hormones which act as natural pain killers. Not only is it super fun, but it also helps alleviate your mood. So, go ahead and enjoy yourself!

6. Mindful Eating

Being mindful of what you eat during your period can significantly impact your symptoms. Incorporating foods rich in iron, like spinach, lentils, and red meat, can help replenish the iron lost due to heavy bleeding. Additionally, eating smaller, more frequent meals can prevent spikes and drops in blood sugar, which can exacerbate mood swings and fatigue during your cycle. Avoiding processed foods, excessive sugar, and caffeine can also help reduce bloating and discomfort.

7. Herbal Supplements

Certain herbal supplements have been traditionally used to manage heavy menstrual bleeding and alleviate associated symptoms. For instance, Vitex (Chasteberry) is known to help balance hormones, while ginger and cinnamon can reduce inflammation and pain. Before starting any herbal supplements, it's essential to consult with a healthcare provider to ensure they are safe and appropriate for your specific needs.

8. Relaxation Techniques

Stress can exacerbate menstrual symptoms, making it essential to find ways to relax and calm your mind. Practices such as deep breathing exercises, meditation, and progressive muscle relaxation can help reduce stress levels, which in turn can ease menstrual cramps and discomfort. Incorporating these techniques into your daily routine can also improve overall mental health and well-being.

9. Heat Therapy

Applying heat to your abdomen is a time-tested remedy for easing menstrual cramps. A heating pad, hot water bottle, or warm bath can help relax the muscles of the uterus, which often contract during menstruation, leading to pain. This simple yet effective remedy can provide instant relief from cramps and can be used as often as needed.

10. Magnesium Intake

Magnesium is a crucial mineral that plays a role in muscle relaxation and may help reduce menstrual cramps. Foods rich in magnesium, such as almonds, pumpkin seeds, and dark chocolate, can be beneficial. Alternatively, a magnesium supplement, taken under the guidance of a healthcare provider, can also help manage cramps and support overall menstrual health.
