NayiDisha Overview

NayiDisha is a transformative initiative aimed at addressing the critical issue of period poverty in rural areas. This project seeks to improve menstrual health and hygiene among women and girls by tackling economic, educational, and infrastructural challenges. By providing access to affordable menstrual products, enhancing sanitation facilities, offering comprehensive menstrual health education, and challenging cultural stigmas, NayiDisha aims to create a supportive and healthy environment for managing menstruation.

Solutions Provided

Empowering communities with knowledge, resources, and support to redefine menstrual narratives and foster an inclusive environment.

  • Affordable Menstrual Products
  • Sanitation Infrastructure Improvement
  • Comprehensive Menstrual Health Education
  • Challenging Cultural Stigmas
  • Training for Local Health Workers
  • Partnerships with Local Organizations
  • Monitoring and Evaluation of Impact
  • Creation of Educational Materials and Resources

Impact on Rural Communities

Women and girls facing economic and infrastructural challenges related to menstrual health include those struggling to afford menstrual products due to financial constraints, as well as communities lacking adequate sanitation facilities, clean water access, and proper disposal systems for menstrual waste.

These individuals are particularly vulnerable to the adverse effects of period poverty, which can lead to unhygienic practices, health risks, and significant educational setbacks. By addressing both economic and infrastructural barriers, NayiDisha aims to support and empower these communities, ensuring they have the resources and facilities needed for managing menstrual health effectively and with dignity.

Project at a Glance

Addressing period poverty and menstrual health challenges across rural areas, aiming to empower women and girls with essential resources and education.

12 Months
