Sakhi Overview

Unveiling the hidden challenges surrounding menstruation, shedding light on disparities, and advocating for change through Sakhi. Our mission is to break the silence and dismantle the stigma surrounding menstruation in rural areas through comprehensive education and community engagement.

Our approach includes community mobilization, the creation of informative and engaging IEC (Information, Education, and Communication) materials, and comprehensive education sessions. These initiatives are designed to create sustainable changes in attitudes and practices regarding menstrual health.

Solutions Provided

Empowering communities with knowledge, resources, and support to redefine menstrual narratives and foster inclusive environment.

  • Formation of Sakhi Educators
  • Production of IEC Materials
  • Regular Awareness Workshops
  • Accessible Menstrual Products
  • Menstrual Health Counseling
  • Community Engagement Programs
  • Peer-to-Peer Education
  • Improved Sanitation Facilities

The Impact of Period Poverty on School Girls

Around 500 million women across the world, including a significant number of school girls, do not have access to private spaces and menstrual products that would enable them to manage their periods in a dignified way. In many regions across the world, this challenge is further aggravated by silence, restrictive sociocultural norms, and taboos around menstruation.

India is no exception to this and still needs to address poor MHMM practices despite government and non-government interventions having increased. The lack of adequate menstrual health management has a profound impact on the education and well-being of school girls.
