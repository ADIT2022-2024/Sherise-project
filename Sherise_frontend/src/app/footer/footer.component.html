About Us

In rural areas, period poverty is a significant issue exacerbated by economic, educational, and infrastructural challenges. Many individuals in these regions face financial constraints that make purchasing menstrual products such as pads, tampons, or menstrual cups unaffordable. This often forces them to resort to using unhygienic alternatives like rags, leaves, or even sand, leading to serious health risks such as infections and reproductive health problems.

The lack of comprehensive menstrual health education further perpetuates misinformation and stigma surrounding menstruation, making it difficult for girls and women to manage their periods effectively.
