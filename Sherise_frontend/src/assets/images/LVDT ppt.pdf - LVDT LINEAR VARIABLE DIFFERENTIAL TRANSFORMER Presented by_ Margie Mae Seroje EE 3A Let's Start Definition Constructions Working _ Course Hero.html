LVDT LINEAR VARIABLE DIFFERENTIAL TRANSFORMER Let's StartPresented by: Margie Mae Seroje EE 3A
Definition Constructions Working Principle Advantages of LVDT Disadvantages of LVDT Industrial ApplicationsLinear Variable Differential Transformer
LVDT Linear Variable Differential Transformer or simply LVDT is an inductive transducer used for measuring the linear displacement.
Construction: LVDT consists of primary and secondary windings Hudson, L. (2019). Factors to Consider When Selecting and Specifying LVDT Linear Position Sensors. Retrieved from Factors to Consider When Selecting and Specifying LVDT Linear Position Sensors - Tech Briefs The primary winding is centered between a pair of identically wound secondary windings Coils are uniformly wound on a thermally stable glass reinforced polymer which is of hollow shape The moving element of the LVDT is called as a core
Working Principle: an alternating current of appropriate amplitude and frequency called as primary excitation is given to the primary winding of LVDT. The electrical output signal from LVDT is obtained as the differential ac voltage between its two secondary windings It is mathematically expressed as: Eo= Es1− Es2 Toiotto, G.
